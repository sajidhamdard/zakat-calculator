# Zakat Calculator

A free online Zakat calculator available at [zakatcalculation.in](https://zakatcalculation.in/).

## What is Zakat?

Zakat is one of the five pillars of Islam. It is a mandatory annual charity that every eligible Muslim must pay on their accumulated wealth. The standard rate is 2.5% of the total zakatable net wealth, calculated once per lunar year (Hawl), provided the wealth stays at or above the minimum threshold known as Nisab.

Zakat is not optional. It is a religious obligation for Muslims whose net wealth meets or exceeds the Nisab threshold for a full lunar year.

## What is ZakatCalculation.in?

ZakatCalculation.in is a free, no-login, web-based tool that helps Muslims calculate their annual Zakat al-Mal (wealth Zakat) quickly and accurately. It supports multiple asset types, currencies, and languages, making it accessible to Muslims worldwide.

The formula used is:

```
Zakat = (Total Zakatable Assets - Payable Liabilities) x 2.5%
```

Zakat is only due if the resulting net wealth is equal to or greater than the Nisab.

## Features

- Calculates Zakat on gold, silver, cash, bank balance, investments, business stock, and property
- Supports both Silver Nisab (612.36 g) and Gold Nisab (87.48 g) methods
- Deduct short-term liabilities and pending bills before calculation
- Option to include previous year's unpaid Zakat
- Available in 10 languages: Hindi, English, Arabic, Spanish, French, Chinese, Bengali, Portuguese, Russian, Urdu
- Supports 11 currencies: INR, USD, EUR, GBP, SAR, AED, PKR, BDT, IDR, CNY, MYR
- Fully mobile responsive
- Completely free with no sign-up required

## How to Use

1. Open [zakatcalculation.in](https://zakatcalculation.in/) in your browser.
2. Select your preferred language and currency from the dropdowns at the top.
3. Enter your cash and bank balance.
4. Enter the weight and current price per gram for gold and silver you own.
5. Enter the value of any investments, business stock, or investment property.
6. Enter any short-term liabilities such as loans due within the year, pending bills, or taxes.
7. If you have any unpaid Zakat from last year, enter that amount as well.
8. Choose the Nisab method (Silver is recommended for mixed assets).
9. Enter the current market price of gold and silver for the Nisab calculation.
10. Click "Calculate" to see your Zakat amount along with a full breakdown.

## What to Include and What to Exclude

**Include in calculation:**
- Cash on hand
- Bank savings and current account balances
- Gold and silver (that you own for more than one lunar year)
- Stocks, mutual funds, and other investments
- Business inventory (stock for sale)
- Property purchased for resale or investment

**Do not include:**
- Your primary home (the house you live in)
- Personal-use vehicles
- Household items and furniture
- Equipment not intended for sale
- Cryptocurrency (opinions vary; consult a scholar)

**Liabilities to deduct:**
- Only short-term debts due within the current lunar year (e.g., next EMI installment, payable bills, outstanding taxes)
- Do not deduct the full outstanding balance of long-term loans

## Nisab

Nisab is the minimum wealth threshold above which Zakat becomes obligatory.

| Nisab Type   | Weight Basis | Notes                                    |
|--------------|--------------|------------------------------------------|
| Silver Nisab | 612.36 g     | Lower threshold, generally recommended  |
| Gold Nisab   | 87.48 g      | Higher threshold                         |

Many scholars recommend using Silver Nisab for mixed assets (cash, gold, and business assets combined) because it has a lower threshold and ensures more people fulfill their obligation.
